Borobudur

Home Gallery About Feedback

Borobudur, located in Central Java, Indonesia, is the largest Buddhist temple in the world. Built in the 9th century during the Sailendra Dynasty, it is designed as a massive mandala representing Buddhist cosmology. The monument consists of nine stacked platforms topped by a central dome, surrounded by 72 stupas each containing a statue of Buddha.

The temple's walls are adorned with over 2,600 relief panels depicting Buddhist teachings and daily life in ancient Java. Pilgrims walk through its terraces in a clockwise direction, symbolizing a spiritual journey from the earthly realm to enlightenment. Abandoned for centuries and later rediscovered, Borobudur has been restored and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It remains both a place of pilgrimage and a symbol of Indonesia's cultural heritage, attracting visitors from around the globe.

  • Location: Central Java, Indonesia
  • Built: 9th century
  • Purpose: Buddhist temple and pilgrimage site
