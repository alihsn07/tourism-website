Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal in Agra, India, is widely regarded as one of the greatest masterpieces of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love. Commissioned in 1632 by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, the mausoleum was constructed over a period of more than 15 years, with thousands of artisans and craftsmen contributing to its creation. Built entirely of gleaming white marble, the Taj Mahal is adorned with intricate carvings, delicate inlay work using semi-precious stones, and verses from the Qur'an inscribed in elegant calligraphy.

The monument's design reflects perfect symmetry, with a central dome flanked by four smaller domes and four slender minarets at each corner. Surrounding the mausoleum are lush gardens and reflecting pools that enhance its beauty, especially at sunrise and sunset when the marble changes color with the light. The Taj Mahal is not only a romantic gesture but also a testament to the artistic and engineering achievements of the Mughal Empire. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it attracts millions of visitors each year who come to admire its breathtaking beauty and learn about the history behind its construction.

  • Location: Agra, India
  • Built: 1632-1648
  • Purpose: Mausoleum for Mumtaz Mahal
